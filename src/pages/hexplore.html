Novel in Progress

Hearth

A surreal literary fantasy about identity, memory, trauma, empathy, power, and the unstable border between fantasy and reality.

About the Novel

A world fractured by war and the uncertainty of everyday life

Hearth follows the twins Vex and Katie as they struggle to understand themselves in a war-torn world shaped by grief, fractured memory, strange powers, and uncertain truths.

Their journey moves between the intimate and the cosmic: childhood stories, damaged relationships, and personal failings; and surreal landscapes, hidden histories, and supernatural forces.

At its center, Hearth asks what it means to become whole when identity itself feels divided, and whether empathy can remain possible in a world built around fear, violence, and control.

Recurring Ideas

Themes

The novel returns to these questions across its characters, settings, and intertwined stories.

Identity

How people construct themselves when memory, expectation, trauma, and desire pull them in different directions.

Memory

The ways memory protects, distorts, traps, and rebuilds people.

Empathy

How understanding others (whether someone has a bad day or a supercosmic threat destroys a planet) always is the solution.

Time

Time is a force that bends, fragments, repeats, and reshapes the meaning of experience and existence.

Fantasy and Reality

The unstable divide between imagination, memory, fantasy, perception, and the physical world.

Hope

The possibility of choosing a direction even when certainty, safety, and simple answers no longer exist.

The People of Hearth

Featured Characters

These introductions are intentionally brief so that the story can reveal the characters gradually.

Twin

Vex

Introspective, uncertain, and deeply affected by the past, Vex searches for meaning while struggling to understand his own mind and the world transforming around him.

Twin

Katie

Katie shares Vex’s history but responds to it in her own way. Her strength, vulnerability, and developing power make her central to the novel’s emotional and moral conflicts.

Companion

Scarlett

Scarlett becomes part of the unstable group surrounding the twins, carrying her own history, loyalties, and understanding of the world’s dangers.

Companion

Ottie

Ottie brings another perspective to the story’s shifting relationships, moral uncertainty, and struggle for survival.

The Setting

A familiar world made strange

The world of Hearth contains ruined landscapes, unstable institutions, dreamlike spaces, and places that appear to exist outside ordinary time.

Its setting is shaped as much by perception as geography. Rooms, forests, roads, memories, and entire worlds can become reflections of the people moving through them.

Behind the Novel

Writing Hearth

Hearth began as an exploration of reflection, fractured identity, fantasy as salvation, and the desire to find truth in a world where certainty may not exist.

The novel combines literary fiction, surrealism, psychological realism, fantasy, and philosophical inquiry. Its form changes with its characters, allowing memories, embedded stories, dreams, and alternate perspectives to reshape the narrative.
